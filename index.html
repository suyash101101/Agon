Overview

Agon: The Archaic Age is a competitive engine-building and battle game set in mythological Greece. Players summon Spirits, recruit Demi-Gods, call upon the twelve Olympians, unleash Monsters, and forge Weapons in pursuit of Patron Points. The player with the most PP when the final round ends wins.

Every God, Demi-God, and Monster card exists as a single copy. Once someone acquires it, it is locked in their engine for the rest of the game. That scarcity is the spine of every decision.

Laps and Rounds

Why laps exist

A round is divided into laps. In each lap, every player takes one full turn (2 actions). After all laps are complete, the round goal is scored and the round ends. Laps give the engine time to compound, give players room to recover from bad draws, and make the game reactive rather than a sequence of independent soliloquies.

Lap order

Turns alternate within each lap. In a 2-player game with 3 laps per round, the sequence inside one round looks like this:

Lap 1: Player 1 takes 2 actions → Player 2 takes 2 actions
Lap 2: Player 1 takes 2 actions → Player 2 takes 2 actions
Lap 3: Player 1 takes 2 actions → Player 2 takes 2 actions
End of Round: Score the goal. Pass first player marker.

The player who holds the first player marker goes first in every lap of that round.

Per round vs on your turn

Per Round means once per round cycle, at Step 1 (engine phase), before anyone takes a lap. It fires exactly once regardless of how many laps there are.

On your turn means during your 2-action turn, each time you take a turn. In a 3-lap round, a player with 3 laps takes 3 turns, so "on your turn" effects could fire up to 3 times in that round.

This distinction matters most for free actions and triggered abilities. When in doubt, ask: does this fire during the engine phase (per round) or during someone's individual turn (on your turn)?

Full round sequence

1
Engines Fire
All players collect resources from their engine simultaneously. Every card in your play area gives its resource automatically. This happens once, before any laps begin.
2
Monster Afflictions Fire
Every deployed monster effect triggers for its target once. This also happens once per round, before any laps. Targets may pay the removal cost here as a free action.
3
Goal Reveal
Flip the top round goal face-up if it is not already visible. Both players see what they are competing for.
4
Laps
Players alternate turns within each lap, for the correct number of laps (see table above). Each turn is 2 actions. Free actions can be used at any point during your own turn.
5
Score the Goal
After the final lap, check the round goal. The qualifying player earns the PP. Apply the tie rule if needed. Discard the goal card.
6
Pass First Player
The marker moves clockwise. Omen Tokens and Glory Track positions carry over.
After the final round, total all PP from every source. Most PP wins. See the Scoring section for the full list.

Resources

🟡
Ambrosia
The common currency. Buy cards, pay costs, convert to PP at the Oracle. Rate: 2 Ambrosia = 1 PP.
🔵
Nectar
The premium resource. Worth more at the Oracle. Mainly from God cards and sea-domain Spirits. Rate: 1 Nectar = 2 PP.
🔴
Ichor
Divine blood. Rare. Deploy monsters, summon Gods, forge weapons, activate powers. Rate: 1 Ichor = 3 PP. You start with none.

Ichor only comes from specific card passives once you build your engine. Until then you have zero. Plan for this early.

Free Actions

These cost no action. Use them at any point during your own turn.

📜
Complete a Quest
Reveal a quest from your hand when its condition is met. Collect the PP. Limit: 2 completed quests per player per game.
✕
Emergency Discard
Discard 1 card from your hand and take 1 Ambrosia. The Phoenix returns to your hand instead of going to discard.
🛡
Remove a Monster
Pay the removal cost printed on a monster that is deployed on you. You then claim it as a trophy (its PP value counts at game end).

Weapon Rules

A weapon card is always in one of two states: equipped (in your engine) or in your hand.

Equipped weapons

You put a weapon into your engine using a Play Card action, the same as any other card. Once equipped, it gives its passive bonus every round in the engine phase. An equipped weapon can also be played in battles (see below), and it returns to your engine unchanged after the battle.

Hand weapons

A weapon kept in your hand gives no passive bonus but can be used in two ways: played face-down in a battle (returns to your hand after), or activated for its one-time powerful effect (discarded after use).

Activating a weapon

Activating is your Colosseum action for that turn. You can activate from hand or from your engine. The weapon is discarded after use either way. You cannot activate a weapon you already played in a battle this same turn.

Dual auto-win: If both players play a weapon with an auto-win effect in the same battle (for example, both play Spear of Ares), treat it as a tie. Both lose 1 Ambrosia. Neither weapon is discarded.

Battle System

Initiating a battle is free. The Colosseum action itself is the cost.

How it works

1. Declare a target opponent.

2. Both of you secretly choose 0 or 1 weapon from your hand or engine. Reveal at the same time.

3. Attacker base damage: 2. Defender base damage: 1. Add weapon bonuses from both sides.

4. Higher total wins. Tie: both lose 1 Ambrosia.

Outcomes

Attacker wins: Steal 2 Ambrosia from defender, gain 1 PP, advance 1 on the Glory Track.

Defender wins: Defender gains 1 PP. Attacker loses 1 Ambrosia to the supply.

Tie: Both lose 1 Ambrosia. No PP awarded.

God and Demi-God cards cannot be targeted. Only resources and engine effects are affected.

Monster Rules

Every Monster card is unique: one copy, consumed on use, gone from the game permanently.

Two ways to use a monster

Instant Use: Play the monster from your hand as your Colosseum action. Its instant effect fires immediately. No Ichor required. The card is discarded. Cannot be countered.

Deploy: Spend 1 Ichor (2 for Typhon) and place the monster face-up in front of any opponent. Put a duration token on it.

How deploy effects work

Deployed monster effects fire once per round at Step 2, before any laps begin. They do not fire again when individual turns come around. A 2-round monster fires twice total: once at Step 2 of the round it was deployed, and once at Step 2 of the following round.

The timing rule that matters most: "Fires once per round at Step 2" applies to every monster in the game. There is no monster whose deploy effect fires per turn. This is the global standard.

Removal and trophies

The target can pay the removal cost during Step 2 (before their first lap) or at any point during their own turn as a free action. When they pay and remove it, they claim the card as a trophy. It counts for its printed PP at game end.

Natural expiry

If the duration runs out without the target paying for removal, the monster is discarded permanently. No trophy for anyone.

Safety rules

  • Maximum 2 monsters on any one player at the same time. Deploying a third requires removing one of the existing two first (that one expires, no trophy).
  • Two monsters with the same type of restriction do not stack. Only the one with longer remaining duration is active.
  • If a player begins their turn with 0 resources and 0 cards in hand, they may declare Sanctuary once per game as a free action: gain 2 Ambrosia from the supply.

Shuffle Spirit, Demi-God, and Monster decks separately. Reveal 4 Spirits, 2 Demi-Gods, and 2 Monsters face-up in the Underworld market.

Shuffle the Weapon deck. Reveal 3 Weapons face-up in the Forge.

Shuffle all 12 God cards face-down. This is its own deck. Gods are summoned by paying Ichor at Hades' domain: reveal top 2, keep 1, return 1 to the bottom.

Shuffle the 8 Round Goal cards. Place face-down. Reveal the top one. That is Round 1's goal.

Quest Cards

Starting Resources
Give each player 4 Ambrosia, 1 Nectar, and 2 Spirit cards dealt from the Spirit deck into hand (not from the market). All players start at 0 on the Glory Track.

Begin

Quick Reference

YOUR TURN — 2 ACTIONS

  • 🌑 Hades: acquire a card (pay its cost)
  • ⚡ Olympus: 2 Ambrosia / 1 Nectar / 1 Ichor (plus engine bonuses)
  • ▶ Play: move card from hand to engine (On Play fires)
  • 🔥 Oracle: score PP, gain 1 Omen Token this turn
  • 🔨 Forge: acquire a weapon (equip or hold)
  • ⚔️ Colosseum: deploy monster / battle / activate weapon

FREE ACTIONS

  • 📜 Complete quest when condition is met
  • ✕ Discard 1 card, gain 1 Ambrosia
  • 🛡 Pay removal cost to remove a monster and claim trophy

SAFETY

  • Max 2 monsters on any one player
  • 0 resources and 0 cards: Sanctuary (once per game) = +2 Ambrosia

ORACLE RATES

  • 2 Ambrosia = 1 PP
  • 1 Nectar = 2 PP
  • 1 Ichor = 3 PP
  • +1 PP per 🔱 engine card per visit
  • 1 Omen Token per turn (3 tokens = +2 PP at game end)

BATTLE

  • Free to initiate (Colosseum action is the cost)
  • Attacker base: 2  ·  Defender base: 1
  • Add weapon bonuses from both sides
  • Win: steal 2 Ambrosia + 1 PP + 1 Glory
  • Lose (attacker): lose 1 Ambrosia to supply
  • Tie: both lose 1 Ambrosia  ·  Dual auto-win = tie

MONSTERS

  • Deploy effect fires once per round at Step 2, before any laps
  • Removal: pay cost to claim trophy
  • Natural expiry: monster discarded, no trophy
