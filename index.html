Overview

Agon: The Archaic Age is a competitive engine-building and battle game set in mythological Greece. Players summon Spirits, recruit Demi-Gods, call upon the Olympian gods, unleash Monsters against rivals, and forge legendary Weapons — all competing for Patron Points.

Every God, Demi-God, and Monster card exists as a single copy in the game. Once claimed, it is locked in that player's engine. This creates true draft tension — there is only one Achilles, one Zeus, one Typhon.

Resources

🟡
Ambrosia
Common currency. Acquire cards, pay costs, convert to PP at Oracle's Fire. Rate: 2 Ambrosia = 1 PP.
🔵
Nectar
Premium resource. Worth more at Oracle's Fire. Mainly from God cards and sea-domain Spirits. Rate: 1 Nectar = 2 PP.
🔴
Ichor
Divine blood. Rare. Deploy monsters, summon gods, forge weapons, activate divine powers. Rate: 1 Ichor = 3 PP.
Ichor note: You begin with 0 Ichor. It comes only from God card passives, Demi-God abilities (Bellerophon, Achilles), Phoenix (odd rounds), and Aether Spirit. Budget it — it gates the most powerful actions in the game.

"Commission the Smith"

"Enter the Fray"

Take exactly 2 actions per turn. Same domain may be visited twice. Engine fires automatically before your first action.

✕

Weapon Rules

Two States: Equipped vs. In Hand

  • In Hand: The weapon is held but not producing. It can be played in battles (as your secret weapon card) and returned to hand after.
  • Equipped (in engine): Placed in your engine using a Play Card action. Provides its passive bonus every round. Can also be played in battles.

Using a Weapon in Battle

  • Both players secretly select 0 or 1 weapon card from either their hand or engine. Reveal simultaneously.
  • Add the weapon's combat bonus to your base damage.
  • After battle: equipped weapons return to engine unchanged. Hand weapons return to hand unchanged.
  • Only Activated effects require the weapon to be discarded.

Activating a Weapon

  • Activating costs 1 Colosseum action.
  • A weapon can be activated from hand OR from your engine.
  • After activation: the weapon is discarded permanently regardless of where it was.
  • You cannot activate a weapon that you used in a battle this same turn.
Auto-Win Conflict: If both players play a weapon with an auto-win effect simultaneously (e.g. both play Spear of Ares), treat the battle as a tie — both players lose 1 Ambrosia to the supply. Neither weapon is discarded.

Battle System

Visit Ares' Blood Colosseum to challenge opponents. Initiating a battle is free — no Ambrosia cost. The Colosseum action itself is the cost.

⚔️ How to Battle

1. Declare a target opponent. Both know a battle is happening.

2. Both secretly choose 0 or 1 weapon card from hand or engine. Reveal simultaneously.

3. Calculate damage: Attacker base = 2. Defender base = 1. Add each weapon's combat bonus to the respective side.

4. Highest total wins. Tie: both lose 1 Ambrosia to supply.

🏆 Outcomes

Attacker wins: Steal 2 Ambrosia from defender. Gain 1 PP. Gain 1 Glory.

Defender wins: Defender gains 1 PP. Attacker loses 1 Ambrosia to supply.

Tie: Both lose 1 Ambrosia to supply. No PP awarded.

Note: God cards and Demi-Gods cannot be targeted or stolen. Only resources and engine effects can be affected by battle outcomes.

Monster Rules

Monsters are unique cards — 1 copy each. Once deployed or used, they are consumed and gone from the game.

Deploying a Monster (Colosseum action)

  • Spend 1 Ichor (or 2 for Typhon). Place the Monster card face-up in front of your target opponent.
  • The monster's Deploy Effect triggers at the start of each of the target's turns for the stated duration.
  • A player may have at most 2 monsters deployed on them simultaneously. If a 3rd is deployed, the deploying player must choose one of the existing 2 to remove first (it expires — discarded, no trophy).
  • If two monsters have the same effect type (e.g. two "cannot visit Hades" effects), only the longer remaining duration applies — effects do not stack.

Monster Removal (Free Action)

  • The afflicted player may pay the Removal Cost listed on the monster during their turn as a Free Action.
  • When removed this way: the target claims the monster as a trophy (its PP value counts at game end).
  • Hero auto-removals (Heracles, Theseus, Perseus, Orpheus) also award the trophy to the target player.

Natural Expiry

  • If a monster's duration expires without the target paying to remove it, the monster is discarded permanently. No trophy is awarded. The card is out of the game.

Instant Use (No Ichor Cost)

  • Instead of deploying, you may play a Monster card from your hand as your Colosseum action for its Instant Use effect. No Ichor required.
  • Instant Use effects cannot be countered or removed — they fire immediately and the card is discarded.
Monster deck depletion: When the Monster deck empties, the 2 Monster slots in Hades' market remain empty permanently. No new monsters can be acquired. Monsters already in play or in hands continue to function normally.

Round Structure

2–4 players: 6 rounds  ·  5–6 players: 5 rounds
1
Engines Fire (Simultaneous, Free)
All players collect resources from every card in their engine simultaneously. This happens automatically before any player takes a turn.
2
Monster Afflictions Trigger
All deployed monster effects fire for their targets. Each target may attempt removal (Free Action) before their own turn begins. Reduce each monster's duration counter by 1 — if it reaches 0, discard it (no trophy).
3
Goal Reveal
Flip the top round goal card face-up. All players can now see the shared competition target for this round.
4
Player Turns (Clockwise)
Each player takes 2 actions. Free actions may be used at any point during your own turn, not during others' turns.
5
Score the Round Goal
The qualifying player earns the stated PP. On a tie, apply the tie rule printed on that goal card. Discard the goal card face-up.
6
Pass First Player Marker
Marker moves clockwise. Omen Tokens and Glory Track positions carry over to the next round.
After the final round: total all PP from every source. Most PP wins. See Scoring for full breakdown and tiebreakers.

Safety Rules — Preventing Deadlocks

These rules prevent players from being completely locked out of meaningful play.

Sanctuary Rule — Escaping Total Resource Lock

If a player begins their turn with 0 resources of every type AND 0 cards in hand, they may declare Sanctuary as a Free Action once per game: gain 2 Ambrosia from the supply immediately. This bypasses all monster afflictions for this single gain only.

Monster Stack Limit

No player may have more than 2 monsters deployed on them simultaneously. If a deployer attempts to place a 3rd monster, they must first remove one of the existing two (that monster is discarded immediately — no trophy for anyone).

Same-Type Monster Stacking

Two monsters with the same type of restriction (e.g. two "cannot visit Hades" effects) do not stack their durations. Only the one with the longer remaining duration is active at any time.

Weapon Deck Empty

If the Weapon deck is exhausted, visiting Hephaestus' Forge grants 1 Ambrosia instead. Players with Hephaestus in their engine still receive his passive cost reduction (applied to future acquisitions if new cards enter somehow via Hermes or other effects).

Demi-God and Monster Deck Empty

When the Demi-God or Monster deck is fully acquired, those 2 market slots in Hades' domain remain empty. The other slots (Spirits, Weapons) still function and refill normally.

Typhon Duration Expiry

Typhon's 3-round duration is reduced each round even if the target cannot remove it. Typhon will always naturally expire after 3 rounds regardless of removal attempts. The target is never permanently locked by any single monster.

Game Setup ~8 minutes

1
Prepare the Board
Place board centrally. Mark all five domains, Glory Track (starts at 0), Domain Mastery track, and Omen Token pool.
2
Hades' Market
Shuffle Spirit (80 cards), Demi-God (8 cards), and Monster (12 cards) decks separately. Reveal 4 Spirits · 2 Demi-Gods · 2 Monsters face-up in the Underworld market.
3
Forge Market
Shuffle Weapon deck (16 cards). Reveal 3 Weapons face-up in Hephaestus' Forge.
4
God Deck
Shuffle all 12 God cards face-down. Place beside Hades' domain. Gods are summoned by paying their Ichor cost — reveal top 2, keep 1, return 1 to bottom of deck.
5
Round Goals
Shuffle all 8 Round Goal cards. Place face-down. Reveal the top one — this is Round 1's goal.
6
Quest Cards
Shuffle quest deck (30 cards). Deal 3 quest cards to each player face-down. Each player keeps 2 and returns 1 to the bottom of the deck face-down.
7
Starting Resources
Give each player: 4 Ambrosia + 1 Nectar + 2 Spirit cards dealt from the Spirit deck into hand (not from the market). All players start at 0 on the Glory Track.
8
Begin
Youngest player takes the First Player Marker. Reveal Round 1's goal card if not already visible. The Agon begins.
