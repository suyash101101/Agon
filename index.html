Your turn
Take exactly 2 actions. You may repeat the same action twice.
⚫
Visit Hades' Domain — Acquire a card
Pay the ambrosia (or nectar) cost shown on any face-up card in the market. Add it to your hand. The market immediately refills from the deck.
Market = 5 spirit cards face-up + 2 god cards face-up, always replenished.
⚡
Visit Zeus' Domain — Gain resources
Gain 2 ambrosia OR 1 nectar (your choice). For each card in your engine with a Zeus symbol (⚡), gain +1 extra ambrosia on top.
This action is always available — no prerequisites. You can always do something.
▶
Play a card — Build your engine
Move a card from your hand to your play area (engine). Trigger its "on play" effect immediately. From next round onwards, it produces resources automatically at round start.
No cost to play — the cost was paid when acquiring at Hades' domain.
🔱
Visit Poseidon's Domain — Score Patron Points
Spend resources to earn PP. Base rates: 3 ambrosia → 1 PP, or 1 nectar → 2 PP. Each Poseidon card (🔱) in your engine improves conversions by +1 PP per use.
You may spend as much as you want in one visit.
📜
Complete a quest — Claim secret PP
Reveal a quest card from your hand if its condition is currently met. Gain the PP shown and place the quest face-up in your score pile. Each player can complete up to 2 quests.
Quest completion is a free action — it does not cost one of your 2 actions.
✕
Emergency — Discard for ambrosia
Discard any 1 card from your hand → gain 1 ambrosia immediately. This is always available as a backup.
Anti-deadlock safety valve. If you ever feel stuck, this gets you moving again.
No deadlock — you always have a legal action

Round goal cards

One revealed per round, visible to all. At round end, whoever qualifies wins the PP. Creates rivalry and shifts priorities mid-game.
"Richest Olympian"
Most ambrosia when the round ends.
Reward: 3 PP (tiebreaker: most nectar)

Round structure
6 rounds. Each round follows the same sequence.
1 · Round start — Engines fire
All players simultaneously collect resources from their engines. Each card in your play area produces its listed resource. This happens before any player takes a turn — automatic and simultaneous.
2 · Goal reveal
If not already visible, flip the top card of the round goal deck face-up. All players can see what they're competing for this round.
3 · Player turns (clockwise)
Starting with the first player, each player takes their 2 actions in turn. Quest cards may be revealed at any time during your turn (free action). Play continues clockwise until all players have gone.
4 · Round end — Score the goal
Check the current round goal card. The qualifying player(s) receive the stated Patron Points. Place the scored goal card in the discard pile.
5 · Pass first player marker
The first player marker passes clockwise. Everyone gets to go first at least once in a 4-player game, and first access to the market matters.
After round 6 — Final scoring
Add all Patron Points: round goals scored + quest cards completed + PP from god cards + PP from spirit cards. Highest total wins. Tiebreaker: most resources remaining.

Shuffled — players don't know what's coming next.

Game setup
Estimated setup time: 5 minutes
1
Place the board. Separate spirit cards and god cards into two face-down shuffled decks.
2
Reveal 5 spirit cards face-up in Hades' domain. Reveal 2 god cards face-up in the god section.
3
Shuffle the 6 round goal cards. Place face-down in the goal slot. Reveal the top one — this is round 1's goal.
4
Shuffle quest cards. Deal 3 quest cards to each player. Each player secretly chooses 2 to keep and returns 1 to the bottom of the deck.
5
Give each player: 3 ambrosia tokens + 2 spirit cards (dealt from the deck, added to hand, not the market).
6
Youngest player takes the first player marker. The game begins!
Recommended component counts
Spirit cards: 40 (15 unique types, 2–3 copies each)
God cards: 16 (8 unique gods, 2 copies each)
Quest cards: 20 (deal 3 per player, extras available)
Round goal cards: 6 (one per round, no repeats)
Ambrosia tokens: 40
Nectar tokens: 20
Patron Point tokens: 50 (or use a track on the board)
Player boards: 4
First player marker: 1
